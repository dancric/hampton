### Scene 3: Capitol Hill (October)
#### Story
As Newport News and the broader Hampton Roads community moves on from the worst of Hurricane Helen’s immediate aftermath, attention turns to Capitol Hill and how Congress should respond to the disaster. As chairman, Representative Chase has control over the next 10 days of the Seapower and Projection Forces subcommittee of the House Armed Services Committee — what should be scheduled?

The heightened national media attention from the hearings has led to rumors that Mayor Wallace and Union President McKinley will seize the spotlight and protest what’s happening in Hampton Roads.

Meanwhile, Admiral Reid has been gathering further information on the future of the shipyard and what the consequences are for Newport News. Reid will revise their request to Congress on the number of carrier contracts.

#### Scene Decision 3-1 (Representative)
Determine how to schedule 10 “working days” of the subcommittee. Each day can be focused on:
* Positive or Negative hearings focused on any character (including yourself)
* Positive or Negative hearings on the shipyard
* Leaving the committee to fundraise for the American Defense Project’s Super PAC, which will offer more Super PAC resources in the fourth and final scene

#### Scene Decision 3-2 (Mayor & Union President)
The Mayor and the Union President independently determine whether to organize a protest in front of Capitol Hill about the situation in Hampton Roads.

#### Scene Decision 3-3 (Admiral)
Designate an intended number of carrier contracts for the shipyard. Can be between zero (0) and three (3). The number can be the same or different from your plan in August (Scene 1) in the Admiral’s Study.
