### Scene 1: Admiral’s Study (August)
#### Story
With the Navy’s installations in Hampton Roads badly damaged following Hurricane Helen, Admiral Reid has driven down from DC to assess the damage directly (avoiding helicopter usage given the presence of search-and-rescue operators still in the air even two weeks after the storm made landfall). The situation is grim, with the car unable to reach the shipyard due to heavy debris on local access roads. The Admiral has set up a small office in one of the Navy’s few undamaged equipment warehouses.

In this first scene, Admiral Reid must decide what recommendation to make to Congress for the next carrier contract. They must also apportion blame for the catastrophe. As in every scene, GBI CEO Brewer will need to determine the company’s target workforce, and Substacker Carmichael will need to assess the future stock price of the company.

#### Scene Decision 1-1 (Admiral)
Designate an intended number of carrier contracts for the shipyard. Can be between zero (0) and three (3).

#### Scene Decision 1-2 (Admiral)
Select the causes of the catastrophe at the shipyard. Can be *any* combination of:
* **SELF BLAME**: “As Chief of Naval Operations, I take personal and professional responsibility for the catastrophe that Helen has wrought on the military installations in Hampton Roads.”
* **NAVY**: “The Navy recognizes that as a service, it has ignored repeated warnings from climate scientists and others about the long-term risks of climate change. Those risks have now become realized, and the service must accept the consequences of its planning mistakes.”
* **CONGRESS**: “While the Navy has long intended to buttress its climate resilience at critical facilities, such efforts have failed to gain attention as high priorities in the country’s appropriations process.”
* **LOCAL GOVERNMENT**: “While the Navy has remained committed to a climate resilience plan for more than a decade, local concerns have regularly thwarted the efforts of the service from building toward long-term climate resilience.”
* **GRIFFITH BARRETT INDUSTRIES**: “The Navy as an institution has long been committed to climate preparedness, but recognizes that many of its private-sector counterparts do not desire the increased capital contributions required for long-term strategic resilience.”
* **ACT OF GOD**: “Unforeseen calamities are a regular part of any complex organization, and while eventualities are regularly and properly assessed, the Navy concludes that Hurricane Helen was an exceptional storm with an exceptional trajectory that caused exceptional consequences.”
