### Scene 4: Twitter (X) Space (November)
#### Story
The election is now a week away, and regardless of outcome, Congress is expected to vote on the carrier contract during the “lame duck” session after the election before new congressmen are sworn into office in January. The growing readership of the *Virginia Stripes* Substack has given it local influence, and Substacker Carmichael has decided to host a live Twitter (X) Space to discuss the issues affecting Hampton Roads. All characters are invited to discuss their topics of interest, and Carmichael will offer commentary.

Meanwhile, Admiral Reid must submit a last-and-final request for the number of carrier contracts to Congress.

#### Scene Decision 4-1 (All Except Substacker)
Each player has time to offer 3 comments to Substacker Carmichael during the livestream. Those arguments can be positive or negative on any individual character, or promotion of the shipyard.

#### Scene Decision 4-2 (Substacker)
Offer a positive, neutral or negative bias to your commentary on every character except yourself.

#### Scene Decision 4-3 (Admiral)
Designate an intended number of carrier contracts for the shipyard. Can be between zero (0) and three (3). The number can be the same or different from your plan in August (Scene 1) in the Admiral’s Study as well as your plan in October (Scene 3) on Capitol Hill. This is the final carrier contract request that will be put to a vote during the lame duck session of Congress and cannot be changed.

#### Recurring Decision (Substacker)
Analyze the information from the discussion and assess what GBI’s next stock price will be.

#### Recurring Decision (CEO)
Determine the number of intended employees at GBI given the information from the scene. This is the final commitment for the workforce before the carrier contract is determined by Congress.
